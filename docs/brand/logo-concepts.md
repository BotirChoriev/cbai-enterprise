# CBAI Logo Concepts

**Document:** Logo Concept Evaluation v1.0  
**Status:** Documentation only — no final SVG assets  
**Requirement:** Exactly three concepts for constitutional review  
**Selection criteria:** Evidence · Balance · Knowledge · Global Intelligence · Trusted Decisions

---

## Evaluation Framework

Each concept is assessed against:

- **CBAI Constitution alignment** — Evidence-first, political neutrality, no forbidden symbolism
- **Enterprise suitability** — Boardroom, Bloomberg-tier gravitas
- **Scalability** — 16px favicon to billboard
- **Recognition** — Distinct without trend dependency
- **Timelessness** — Durable 10+ year horizon

---

## Concept A — The Foundation Mark

### Description

A **solid horizontal baseline** (evidence layer) supports a **perfect circle** (knowledge node) centered above its midpoint. A **single vertical stroke** bisects the circle and extends equal distance above and below, terminating at the baseline and a short apex point above the circle. The circle is intersected but not divided—the vertical axis represents balance through equilibrium, not opposition.

Wordmark **CBAI** set in geometric sans to the right, cap height aligned with circle diameter. Mark and wordmark separated by one circle-diameter of clear space.

**Construction:** 24×24 grid. Baseline occupies bottom 4 units. Circle diameter 10 units, centered horizontally. Vertical stroke 1 unit width. Apex point 1×1 unit square centered on axis, 2 units above circle.

### Meaning

- **Baseline** = Evidence must ground all intelligence
- **Circle** = Complete knowledge unit, bounded and auditable
- **Vertical axis** = Balance between signals; neutral mediation
- **Apex point** = Trusted decision output—singular, precise, not sprawling

The form reads as **monumental and architectural**—a plinth supporting a lens or datum point.

### Why It Follows CBAI Constitution

- No figurative AI, political, or national symbolism
- Geometry expresses methodology, not personality
- Symmetry communicates political neutrality
- Restrained single-color application supports transparency
- Circle bounded by evidence line enforces Golden Rule visually

### Advantages

- Extremely scalable; reads clearly at 16px (baseline + dot + vertical)
- Highest timelessness potential—recalls civic and scientific monument marks
- Easiest to enforce across strict brand guidelines
- Strong monochrome reproduction for PDF and print
- Distinct from generic tech "node network" logos

### Disadvantages

- May feel austere or cold to consumer audiences (acceptable for enterprise)
- Less immediate "global" read without supporting wordmark
- Circle marks are common—differentiation relies on baseline + apex composition
- Requires careful spacing; cramped layouts weaken meaning

### Scalability

| Size | Readability |
|------|-------------|
| 16px | Good — simplify to baseline + circle + apex dot |
| 24px | Excellent |
| 512px+ | Excellent — full construction visible |

### Recognition

Moderate-high after repeated enterprise exposure. The baseline-circle-axis triad is **learnable and ownable** with consistent application. Unlikely to be confused with consumer AI brands.

### Enterprise Suitability

**Excellent.** Comparable gravitas to Bloomberg, McKinsey institutional marks, and Stripe wordmark discipline. Appropriate for government-adjacent and investor contexts without implying official endorsement.

## Concept B — The Meridian Mark

### Description

Two **parallel vertical lines** (meridians) frame a **central negative-space channel** forming a subtle **leaf or lens shape** through curvature of inner edges only at the vertical midpoint. The inner curves bow inward 1 unit on a 24-unit grid, suggesting convergence without meeting. A **horizontal evidence tick** crosses both meridians at the lower third—extending 2 units beyond each meridian, same stroke weight as meridians.

No enclosed circle. The negative space between curves reads as **knowledge aperture**—what is seen depends on evidence framing.

Wordmark **CBAI** below mark, centered, tracking +40 for institutional letterspacing.

### Meaning

- **Meridians** = Global coordinate frame without drawing a globe
- **Central aperture** = Knowledge focused through disciplined scope
- **Inward curves** = Balance—competing perspectives converging toward center
- **Evidence tick** = Grounding measurement; data precedes interpretation

Evokes **surveying, cartography, and precision instruments**—global intelligence as measurement, not opinion.

### Why It Follows CBAI Constitution

- Global reference without flags or map politics
- Negative space avoids literal icons (no brain, no chat)
- Parallel lines suggest equality and neutrality
- Instrument metaphor supports evidence-first narrative
- Achievable in single color without gradient

### Advantages

- Strong "global intelligence" semantic without cliché globe icon
- Unique silhouette among enterprise SaaS marks
- Horizontal tick creates memorable asymmetric anchor
- Works well in report headers and PDF cover bands
- Pairs naturally with monospace data typography

### Disadvantages

- Inner curves may lost at very small sizes (favicon requires simplified meridians-only)
- Negative-space read requires viewer literacy—less instant than Concept A
- Parallel verticals risk collision with existing brands (II, pause symbols) if poorly spaced
- Below-mark wordmark layout uses more vertical space in sidebar

### Scalability

| Size | Readability |
|------|-------------|
| 16px | Fair — use meridians + tick only; omit curves |
| 24px | Good |
| 512px+ | Excellent — full aperture visible |

### Recognition

High distinctiveness at medium-large sizes; moderate at favicon. Strong recognition in **document and report contexts** where horizontal layout dominates.

### Enterprise Suitability

**Very good.** Instrument-grade seriousness suits intelligence and compliance products. Slightly more distinctive than Concept A but requires more brand education for mark-only recognition.

## Concept C — The Ledger Mark

### Description

Three **horizontal lines** of equal length, left-aligned, stacked with equal vertical spacing—representing **evidence layers** (source, validation, record). The top line is full width (16 units). The middle line is 12 units. The bottom line is 8 units, creating a **staircase or ledger taper** reading bottom-to-top as accumulation toward decision. A **single vertical bracket** on the right connects all three lines, open toward the mark, suggesting audit containment without enclosure.

Optional: one **small square dot** (1 unit) at the right terminus of the top line—decision output.

Monogram **C** formed by negative space between bracket and middle line is **not intended**—avoid literal letter hunting; form stays abstract.

Wordmark **CBAI** to the right, vertically centered on middle line.

### Meaning

- **Three lines** = Evidence strata; nothing skipped in the chain
- **Decreasing width upward** = Distillation—more evidence narrows to finer conclusions (read upward as refinement, not hierarchy of importance)
- **Bracket** = Audit trail, transparency, accountable boundary
- **Terminal dot** = Single trusted decision point

Reads as **accounting ledger, evidence log, or structured record**—core to CBAI epistemic model.

### Why It Follows CBAI Constitution

- Literal representation of evidence layering—Golden Rule compliant
- No political, religious, or AI cliché forms
- Bracket suggests transparency and reviewability
- Abstract geometry avoids country or institution mimicry
- Naturally supports "Insufficient Evidence" brand honesty (incomplete ledger = fewer lines in animation state—future motion, not in static mark)

### Advantages

- Strongest direct semantic tie to CBAI intelligence pipeline
- Excellent horizontal layout for dashboards and wide headers
- Highly legible at small sizes—lines survive 16px
- Natural pairing with evidence status UI patterns already in platform
- Easiest to animate meaningfully (lines appear sequentially) without sci-fi effects

### Disadvantages

- Less emotionally distinctive—may read as "generic data" without wordmark
- Weakest "global intelligence" signal of the three; more local/structural
- Bracket + lines risk similarity to checklist or menu icons if scaled poorly
- Requires wordmark for full brand recognition in early market phase

### Scalability

| Size | Readability |
|------|-------------|
| 16px | Excellent — three lines + bracket simplified |
| 24px | Excellent |
| 512px+ | Good — benefits from wordmark alongside |

### Recognition

Moderate alone; **high with wordmark**. Best recognition in **product UI context** where users already associate lines with evidence blocks. May need 6–12 months exposure for mark-only recall.

### Enterprise Suitability

**Excellent for platform; good for external brand.** Ideal for dashboard, observability, and country intelligence modules. Slightly less premium on blank letterhead than Concept A without supporting typography system.

## Recommendation (Documentation Only)

**Primary recommendation for constitutional approval:** **Concept A — The Foundation Mark** for master brand and external identity; **Concept C — The Ledger Mark** as optional product sub-mark for intelligence runtime and evidence modules.

**Concept B** reserved for report and global intelligence product lines if a secondary mark is approved in v1.1.

Final selection requires stakeholder sign-off before SVG production phase. No asset files are included in this document by design.
